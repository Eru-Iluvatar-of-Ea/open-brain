# Open Brain Guide — Table of Contents

Chapter slugs and scope sentences. Checkpoints tag against these; the compile
merges by tag. A checkpoint proposes a new slug only when no scope sentence
below covers its content.

- **memory-architecture** — How Open Brain fits into the layered memory system: push vs pull retrieval, Layer 1 user-edits vs Open Brain division of labor, retrieval mechanics (top-k, thresholds, discretionary tool invocation), and sizing characteristics.
- **pipeline-mechanics** — How a thought physically moves through the system: capture, embedding generation, metadata extraction, storage, vector search semantics, timestamps, and verified pipeline behavior.
- **capture-strategy** — What to put in and what to keep: granularity rules, retention vs deletion policy, corrective-entry patterns, and curation discipline.
- **extending-the-system** — Building on the primitive: adding MCP tools (update/delete and beyond), patterns to follow, and open build decisions.
